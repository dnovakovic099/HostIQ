# Capturing Media Guide

Learn how to take high-quality photos and videos for inspections that will get the best AI analysis results.

## Overview

Good media is essential for accurate AI analysis. This guide covers best practices for capturing photos and videos during inspections.

## Camera Basics

### Accessing Camera

1. **During Inspection**
   - Start or continue inspection
   - Navigate to room
   - Tap camera button
   - Camera opens

2. **Permissions**
   - First time: Grant camera permission
   - Settings: Can change later
   - Required: For inspections

### Camera Interface

**Controls:**
- **Capture Button**: Take photo
- **Switch Camera**: Front/back (if available)
- **Flash**: On/off/auto
- **Gallery**: View taken photos
- **Close**: Return to inspection

## Photo Best Practices

### Coverage (Most Important!)

**Goal: 80%+ of room visible**

**How to Achieve:**
- Stand in corner of room
- Capture 2-3 walls in frame
- Show floor and ceiling
- Include major furniture

**Bad Coverage:**
- ❌ Close-up of one item
- ❌ Only one wall visible
- ❌ Too zoomed in
- ❌ Missing major areas

**Good Coverage:**
- ✅ Multiple walls visible
- ✅ Most furniture shown
- ✅ Floor clearly visible
- ✅ Room context clear

### Lighting

**Natural Light:**
- ✅ Best option when available
- ✅ Open curtains/blinds
- ✅ Use windows for light

**Artificial Light:**
- ✅ Turn on all lights
- ✅ Use flash if needed
- ✅ Avoid harsh shadows

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Backlit photos (too dark)
- ❌ Harsh direct sunlight
- ❌ Very dim lighting
- ❌ Uneven lighting

### Focus & Clarity

**Getting Clear Photos:**
- Hold device steady
- Wait for auto-focus
- Tap screen to focus
- Ensure good lighting
- Clean camera lens

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Blurry photos
- ❌ Out of focus
- ❌ Camera shake
- ❌ Dirty lens

### Angles & Composition

**Recommended Angles:**

1. **Corner Shots** (Best)
   - Stand in corner
   - Show 2-3 walls
   - Wide room view
   - Best coverage

2. **Wide Shots**
   - Entire room visible
   - Good overview
   - Shows layout

3. **Detail Shots** (When Needed)
   - Specific issues
   - Close-up problems
   - Damage documentation
   - After wide shots

**Composition Tips:**
- Keep camera level
- Avoid extreme angles
- Show room naturally
- Include context

## What to Capture

### Always Include

**Room Overview:**
- Entire room view
- All major furniture
- Floor and surfaces
- Windows and doors

**Key Areas:**
- Entry points
- Main furniture
- Surfaces (counters, tables)
- Floors and baseboards

**Problem Areas:**
- Any issues visible
- Damage or wear
- Clutter
- Out-of-place items

### Room-Specific Guidelines

**Bedroom:**
- Bed (made/unmade)
- Furniture surfaces
- Floor (visible areas)
- Closets (if open)
- Windows

**Bathroom:**
- All fixtures
- Surfaces (counters, floors)
- Mirrors
- Shower/tub
- Toilet area

**Kitchen:**
- Appliances
- Countertops
- Sink area
- Floors
- Cabinets (if relevant)

**Living Room:**
- Seating areas
- Entertainment center
- Surfaces
- Floor
- Windows

## Number of Photos

### Recommended Amount

**Per Room:**
- **Minimum**: 1 photo (wide coverage)
- **Recommended**: 3-5 photos
- **Maximum**: No strict limit

**Breakdown:**
- 1-2 wide shots (coverage)
- 1-2 corner shots (angles)
- 0-2 detail shots (issues)

### When to Take More

**Take Extra Photos If:**
- Room is large
- Multiple areas
- Issues found
- Poor initial coverage
- Complex layout

## Video Capture

### When to Use Video

**Good Uses:**
- Room walkthrough
- Showing functionality
- Demonstrating issues
- Providing context
- Quick overview

**Not Necessary:**
- Standard inspections
- Small rooms
- Simple documentation
- Photos sufficient

### Video Guidelines

**Length:**
- **Recommended**: 10-30 seconds
- **Maximum**: 2 minutes
- **Focus**: Show room comprehensively

**Content:**
- Start at entry
- Pan across room
- Show all areas
- End with overview

**Quality:**
- Steady movement
- Good lighting
- Clear audio (if narrating)
- Smooth panning

## Reviewing & Managing Photos

### During Capture

**Review Photos:**
- Check after each capture
- Verify clarity
- Ensure coverage
- Delete if needed

**Retake If:**
- Photo is blurry
- Poor coverage
- Bad lighting
- Missing areas

### Photo Management

**Viewing:**
- Tap photo to view full size
- Swipe to see all
- Check thumbnails

**Deleting:**
- Swipe or tap delete
- Confirm removal
- Can retake immediately

**Reordering:**
- (If available) Drag to reorder
- Organize logically
- Group by area

## Upload Process

### During Inspection

**Background Upload:**
- Photos upload automatically
- Can continue while uploading
- Progress indicator shown
- Retry if fails

### After Capture

**Review All:**
- See all uploaded photos
- Verify completeness
- Check quality
- Add more if needed

**Before Submission:**
- Review entire set
- Ensure all rooms covered
- Verify photo quality
- Check upload status

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Coverage Issues

❌ **Too Close:**
- Only one item visible
- Missing room context
- Poor for analysis

✅ **Fix:** Step back, wider shot

❌ **One Angle:**
- Only front view
- Missing other walls
- Incomplete coverage

✅ **Fix:** Multiple angles, corner shots

### Quality Issues

❌ **Blurry:**
- Camera shake
- Poor focus
- Low light

✅ **Fix:** Steady hand, good light, wait for focus

❌ **Too Dark:**
- Insufficient lighting
- Backlit
- No flash

✅ **Fix:** Add light, use flash, better positioning

### Content Issues

❌ **Missing Areas:**
- Not showing all of room
- Skipping corners
- Missing furniture

✅ **Fix:** Systematic coverage, check all areas

❌ **Wrong Focus:**
- Focusing on wrong things
- Missing issues
- Incomplete documentation

✅ **Fix:** Follow checklist, be thorough

## Tips for Success

### Before Starting

1. **Prepare:**
   - Charge phone
   - Clean camera lens
   - Check storage space
   - Ensure good internet

2. **Plan:**
   - Know room layout
   - Identify key areas
   - Plan photo sequence

### During Capture

1. **Be Systematic:**
   - Follow room order
   - Cover all areas
   - Don't rush

2. **Check Quality:**
   - Review after each photo
   - Retake if needed
   - Ensure coverage

3. **Document Issues:**
   - Take detail shots
   - Show problems clearly
   - Multiple angles if needed

### After Capture

1. **Review:**
   - Check all photos
   - Verify coverage
   - Ensure quality

2. **Add More:**
   - If coverage poor
   - If issues found
   - If missing areas

## Troubleshooting

### Camera Won't Open

**Solutions:**
- Check permissions
- Restart app
- Restart device
- Update app

### Photos Are Blurry

**Solutions:**
- Clean lens
- Better lighting
- Steady hand
- Wait for focus
- Use flash

### Can't Upload

**Solutions:**
- Check internet
- Try Wi-Fi
- Retry upload
- Restart app

### Storage Full

**Solutions:**
- Delete old photos
- Free up space
- Use cloud backup
- Clear cache
